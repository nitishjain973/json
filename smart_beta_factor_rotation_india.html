The 6 quantitative signals — compute every last Sunday of the month
Signal 1 — Nifty 50 trend
S1 = Nifty50_Close vs 200-DMA Bull: Close > 200DMA × 1.02 Bear: Close < 200DMA × 0.98 Choppy: between these bands
Signal 2 — Nifty 200 breadth
S2 = % of Nifty 200 stocks above their own 50-DMA Bull: S2 > 60% Bear: S2 < 35% Choppy: 35–60%
Signal 3 — India VIX level
S3 = India VIX monthly close Bull: VIX < 15 Choppy: VIX 15–22 Bear: VIX > 22
Signal 4 — Momentum ETF drawdown
S4 = MOM30IETF drawdown from its own 52-week high Risk-on: Drawdown < 10% Caution: Drawdown 10–20% Risk-off: Drawdown > 20%
Signal 5 — Alpha ETF 3M return
S5 = ALPHA ETF 3-month trailing return Strong: S5 > +8% Neutral: S5 -3% to +8% Weak: S5 < -3%
Signal 6 — FII flow
S6 = FII net equity flows trailing 3-month sum (NSE data) Positive: > ₹0 (net buyer) Negative: < ₹0 (net seller) Extreme: < -₹10,000 Cr/mth
Regime classification rule — compute monthly
BULL
ALL of these must be true: S1 = Bull AND S2 > 60% AND S3 < 15 AND S4 < 10% AND S5 > +8% AND S6 positive. If all 6 pass → confirmed Bull regime.
BEAR
ANY 3 of these must be true: S1 = Bear AND/OR S2 < 35% AND/OR S3 > 22 AND/OR S4 > 20% AND/OR S5 < -3% AND/OR S6 extreme negative. 3+ triggers → Bear regime.
CHOPPY
Neither Bull nor Bear: All other combinations. Default state when signals are mixed. The system defaults to Choppy when in doubt — protects from overtrading.
LOCK
Regime change requires 2 consecutive monthly confirmations. If Bull→Bear signal fires in month 1, recheck month 2 before acting. Prevents false signals and whipsaws. Exception: Bear trigger with S3 > 30 (panic VIX) — act immediately without wait.

Bull → Choppy transition rules
SW1
Trigger: 2 consecutive months where S1 drops to Choppy band OR S2 falls below 50% OR VIX crosses above 18.
SW2
Action: Sell MOM30 down from 40% → 20%. Sell ALPHA down from 35% → 20%. Buy ALPL30 up to 40%. Buy ETF D up to 20%.
SW3
Execution: Do NOT sell all at once. Reduce 50% of the planned change in week 1, remaining 50% in week 3. Over 3-week window.
Choppy → Bear transition rules
SW4
Trigger: Any 3 Bear signals fire simultaneously OR S3 (VIX) crosses 25 (single trigger, no wait required).
SW5
Action: Sell MOM30 to 0% and ALPHA to 0% within 5 trading days. Raise ETF D to 70%. This is the most important rule — momentum bleeds in bear markets.
SW6
Execution: Sell MOM30 and ALPHA over 3 days (1/3 each day). Prevents market impact on large switches. Buy ETF D simultaneously.
Bear → Choppy (recovery) transition rules
SW7
Trigger: 2 consecutive months where S1 returns to Choppy band AND S2 > 40% AND VIX < 20 AND S4 drawdown stabilises (MOM30 stops making new lows).
SW8
Action: Move to Choppy allocation first — do NOT jump straight to Bull even if all signals look green. The 2-step recovery (Bear→Choppy→Bull) prevents premature re-entry into volatile assets.
SW9
Alpha/Momentum re-entry: When MOM30IETF closes above its own 200-DMA AND ALPHA ETF 1-month return turns positive — begin adding MOM30 and ALPHA in 10% increments monthly, not all at once.
Choppy → Bull (full risk-on) transition rules
SW10
Trigger: ALL 6 signals simultaneously bullish for 2 consecutive months. No exceptions — all 6 must be green.
SW11
Action: Raise MOM30 to 40%, ALPHA to 35%. Reduce ALPL30 to 20%, ETF D to 5%. This maximises capture of the early bull phase which is historically the best return period.
RB1
Mandatory semi-annual rebalance: Every first week of June and December, regardless of regime. Compare actual allocation vs. regime target. Rebalance if any ETF drifts more than ±7 percentage points from target.
RB2
Monthly drift check: Every last Sunday of month, calculate allocation percentages. If any ETF exceeds target by >10 pp — trigger immediate rebalance. This catches momentum ETF runups in bull phases.
RB3
Within-regime rebalancing formula: If MOM30 allocation drifts from 40% → 50% in Bull (due to outperformance), sell the excess 10% and distribute equally to ALPL30 and ETF D. Lock in gains systematically.
RB4
Tax-aware rebalance order: First — redirect new SIP contributions to underweight ETFs. Second — sell units held for >12 months (LTCG rate). Third — sell short-term holdings only if the drift exceeds 15 pp (drift too large to correct via SIP alone).
RB5
LTCG harvest rule: Every March, sell and immediately repurchase up to ₹1.25L of gains across all ETFs. This resets cost basis tax-free under the annual LTCG exemption. Priority: sell highest-gain ETF first.
RB6
Post-regime-switch rebalance: After any regime switch, wait 10 trading days before conducting any additional rebalance. Let the switch settle and prices stabilise before fine-tuning.
SIP pause rules — when to stop SIP contributions
P1
Pause SIP into MOM30 (ETF A) when: S4 (MOM30 drawdown from 52W high) exceeds 15% OR regime switches to Bear. Resume when S4 recovers below 10% AND regime is Choppy or Bull.
P2
Pause SIP into ALPHA (ETF B) when: S5 (ALPHA 3M return) falls below -5% OR regime is Bear. Never stop SIP into ALPHA in Choppy regime — it often marks near the bottom.
P3
NEVER pause SIP into ETF C (ALPL30) under any condition. This is your all-weather ETF. Continuous accumulation here provides the rebalancing pool in Bear markets.
P4
During Bear regime: Redirect all paused SIP amounts (from A and B) into ETF D (Qual/EW). Do not reduce total SIP amount — redirect, not stop. You buy cheap quality during bear markets.
Resume rules — when to restart stopped SIPs
R1
Resume MOM30 SIP when: Regime confirms Choppy for 2 consecutive months AND MOM30 closes above its own 100-DMA AND S3 (VIX) falls below 20.
R2
Resume ALPHA SIP when: S5 (3M return) turns positive AND regime is Choppy or Bull AND India VIX is below 18.
R3
Resume at 50% of normal SIP amount for the first month after any Bear→Choppy switch. Restore to 100% the following month if signals remain stable. Prevents over-committing during false recoveries.

Lump sum crash rule: If Nifty 50 falls >20% from its all-time high in any 3-month window, deploy additional lump sum (50% into ALPL30, 50% into ETF D) in 3 tranches over 6 weeks. Never into MOM30 during a crash — momentum ETFs compound losses.

Annual SIP step-up: Increase SIP by minimum 10% every April. Compound SIP growth, not just portfolio growth.

Rules you must never break

Never hold MOM30 or ALPHA in a confirmed Bear regime. Momentum factors bleed catastrophically in bear markets — they own the stocks that were recently strong (often the most overvalued). The 2008 and 2020 crashes show MOM30-equivalent strategies fell 55–65% versus Nifty's 38–50%.

Never switch regime on a single month's signal. The 2-month confirmation rule exists precisely because a single bad VIX reading or FII outflow day can trigger a false alarm. Whipsaws cost more than the drawdown they prevent.

Never stop SIP entirely. Even in Bear regime, redirect to ETF D. Stopping SIP is the number-one way to miss the recovery that always follows every Indian bear market.

Never rebalance back to momentum during a crash rally. Bear market rallies of 10–15% are common. Wait for 2-month Choppy confirmation before re-adding MOM30 and ALPHA. The rally may be a dead-cat bounce.

Always use limit orders when switching ETF positions. Smart beta ETFs have wider bid-ask spreads than plain Nifty ETFs. A market order on MOM30IETF for large amounts can move your own execution price 0.3–0.5% against you.

Monthly monitoring checklist — 30 minutes per month
1
Last Sunday of month: Pull Nifty 50 closing price and its 200-DMA from Investing.com or TradingView. Compute S1.
2
Pull India VIX monthly close from NSE website. Compute S3.
3
Pull MOM30IETF price from NSE. Compare to its 52-week high. Compute S4 (drawdown %).
4
Pull ALPHA ETF 3-month trailing return from INDmoney or Kuvera. Compute S5.
5
Pull FII monthly net equity flow from NSE website (market data → FII/DII data). Compute 3-month sum for S6.
6
Score all 6 signals. Determine if regime has changed. If yes — is this 2nd consecutive month of the same new regime? If yes → execute switch per rules SW1–SW11.
7
Check current portfolio allocation vs. regime target. If any ETF drifted >7pp → note for June/December rebalance. If >10pp → immediate rebalance.
8
Confirm next month SIP allocation matches current regime. Transfer correct amounts.
